# Product Guidelines

## Prose Style
Documentation and user-facing content should adopt a **Technical and Precise** prose style. The focus must be on accuracy, clarity, and detail, making it suitable for scientific and engineering audiences. Jargon should be used appropriately within the context of CFD and porous media, with clear definitions provided where necessary. The language should be unambiguous and objective, reflecting the technical nature of the project.

## Tone and Voice
External communications, including but not limited to release notes, academic papers, and project updates, should maintain a **Professional and Authoritative** tone and voice. This approach aims to convey expertise, reliability, and scientific rigor. While professional, communications should also strive to be clear and informative, ensuring that the project's advancements and capabilities are presented effectively to the target audience.

## Visual Identity and Branding
The project's visual identity and branding should adhere to a **Functional and Clean** design philosophy. This means prioritizing clarity, usability, and a minimalist aesthetic in all graphical representations, user interfaces, and diagrams. Visuals should be straightforward, easy to interpret, and directly support the technical content without unnecessary embellishment. Emphasis should be placed on presenting data and concepts in a clear, unambiguous manner.
